    Japanese Verb Conjugation Cheat Sheet

    Japanese verbs are categorized into three groups:

    • Group 1 (Godan verbs): Verbs ending with consonant sounds.
    • Group 2 (Ichidan verbs): Verbs ending with the syllable "iru" or "eru".
    • Irregular verbs: Only two verbs, "する (suru)" and "来る (kuru)".

    Conjugation Patterns:

    To conjugate Group 1 verbs, change the final syllable according to the desired form:

    Form Conjugation Rule Example (書く - kaku)
    Te-form Change final 'ku' to 'ite' 書いて (kaite)
    Past Positive Change final 'ku' to 'ita' 書いた (kaita)

    For Group 2 verbs, drop the 'る' and add the appropriate ending:

    Form Conjugation Rule Example (食べる - taberu)
    Te-form Add 'て' 食べて (tabete)
    Past Positive Add 'た' 食べた (tabeta)

    Irregular verbs have unique conjugations:

    Verb Te-form Past Positive
    する (suru) して (shite) した (shita)
    来る (kuru) 来て (kite) 来た (kita)
